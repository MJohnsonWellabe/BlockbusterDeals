# Blockbuster Deals — Reinsurance Model

A browser-based actuarial model for evaluating a **quota-share reinsurance treaty** on a
Medicare Supplement block, from the **ceding company's** point of view. It answers one
question: when is the deal worth doing, and at what structure? All computation runs in the
browser via Pyodide (Python/WASM) — there is no server.

## 1. Overview

- The cedant reinsures a percentage of its in-force and new Med Supp business. The reinsurer
  takes that share of premium, claims and reserves, pays **ceding commissions** (an upfront
  front-end payment plus an ongoing sliding-scale allowance), and in return relieves the cedant
  of required capital and early-year earnings strain.
- The model projects three views and compares them:
  - **Predeal** — the book with no reinsurance (the baseline).
  - **Ceded** — the slice handed to the reinsurer, net of commissions paid back.
  - **Net** — what the cedant retains after the deal (Predeal minus Ceded, plus commissions).
- It is internally zero-sum: **Predeal PVDE = Net PVDE + Ceded PVDE**. The ceded value is exactly
  the profit the cedant gives up; the question is whether the capital and strain relief are worth it.
- The treaty preserves a **one-year cession lag**: business issued in a policy year is reinsured
  starting the following calendar year.

## 2. Inputs

### EV Projection Data
- A 30-year monthly economic-value projection, keyed by issue year. The built-in default is a
  slimmed file collapsed across product and new-business, preserving aggregate premium and claim
  totals. The `<=2019` bucket is the back book (read as issue year 2019); 2020-2030 are explicit.
- Upload your own CSV on the Upload tab to override the default.

### RBC & Surplus
- Required-capital and surplus inputs (NAIC covariance charges, target surplus, TAC) drive the
  RBC ratios and the capital-relief calculation. A full-precision default set is built in.

### Balance Plan
- Optional plan figures used only on the Validation tab to reconcile the model against plan.

## 3. Assumptions (Assumptions tab)

### Treaty Structure (the deal levers)
- **Reinsurance %** by issue year x calendar year (the cession matrix; lower triangle = pre-issue).
- **Upfront (front-end) ceding commission** — a cash payment by the reinsurer, by calendar year.
- **Ongoing ceding commission** — a sliding scale that pays **less** as the loss ratio rises
  (`$250 / $200 / $150 / $100` per policy/year across LR bands by default). This is
  reinsurer-protective: the cedant's commission income falls in bad-claims worlds.

### Experience (stress)
- **Claim scalar** and **lapse scalar** flex incurred claims and lapse rates.
- **Sales scalar** by issue year flexes new-business volume.

### Economic
- **Discount rate** for PVDE, **Cost of Capital** for valuing capital relief, **premium tax**,
  and the **base calendar year**.

## 4. How the model computes

### Income statement
- Monthly per-cohort income statements roll up to annual: premium, net investment income, claims,
  reserve changes, commissions, premium tax, acquisition and maintenance expense, pre-tax income.

### Distributable earnings & PVDE
- **Distributable earnings (DE)** = after-tax pre-tax income less the change in target surplus.
- **PVDE** discounts DE at the discount rate; **IRR** is the return on the DE stream.
- **max_neg_cum_de** is the deepest point of cumulative DE — the worst early-year capital strain.

### Ceded & Net
- The cession matrix applies the reinsurance % (with the one-year lag) to produce the Ceded view;
  Net = Predeal minus Ceded plus the ceding commissions paid back to the cedant.

### RBC & Surplus
- Required capital and the post-covariance RBC ratio are recomputed for Original, Predeal and Net.
  The deal **lifts** the RBC ratio by freeing required capital — that lift is the capital benefit.

## 5. Results tabs

- **Summary** — the cedant-economics headline (Section 6) plus 5-year income statements. Default view.
- **Income Statement** — Predeal / Ceded / Net behind one toggle (annual or monthly, with CSV).
- **RBC & Surplus** — Original / Predeal / Net / Lift.
- **Data** — browse the raw EV input rows (direct and ceded).
- **Validation** — Balance Plan reconciliation, Issue-Year diagnostic, Test Log, and a data-integrity checklist.
- **Frontier** — the deal-structure efficient frontier (Section 7).

## 6. Cedant Economics (Summary metrics)

All in $M unless noted. **What you give up:**
- **Ceded PVDE** — profit (PV of distributable earnings) handed to the reinsurer.
- **IRR change** — book IRR before vs after the deal; the return given up for the relief.

**What you get:**
- **Upfront commission** — front-end ceding commission paid by the reinsurer, shown as a schedule
  of $M by year (e.g. 10-5-5 = $10M/$5M/$5M in 2026/2027/2028). It is a component of the
  early-strain relief below.
- **Early-strain relief** — reduction in the peak negative cumulative earnings: early capital you
  no longer have to hold (includes the upfront commission).
- **RBC ratio lift** — improvement in the RBC ratio at the trough year (regulatory capital freed).
- **Cedant recovery %** — commissions received as a share of the value ceded; already netted out of
  the Ceded PVDE above, so it is not additive to it.
- **Portfolio EV** — total book value of new business (PV distributable earnings) before vs after
  the deal.
- **Back-book / new-issue IRR** — IRR of in-force (<=2025) vs new business (>=2026), each shown
  before vs after the deal, so you can see whether you are ceding your healthiest business.

## 7. Deal Analysis

Most reinsurance levers are **monotonic** for the cedant — more upfront cash and a higher ongoing
commission are always better — so this is not an efficient-frontier question. Instead the Deal
Analysis tab quantifies the **cost of each assumption** and flags **where the deal turns bad**.
From a base deal you set (cede %, ongoing level, upfront schedule, years of new business), each
lever is varied **one at a time** and three panels are produced:

1. **Cost of each assumption** — a tornado of how much net portfolio EV swings as each lever moves
   across its range (others held at base), plus a detailed table per lever (new-business net IRR,
   new-business and back-book net EV, back-book compensation %, ceded PVDE, RBC lift, strain relief,
   net EV). The longest bars are the assumptions worth negotiating hardest.
2. **New-business IRR vs cede %** — new-issue net IRR as cession rises, against your hurdle (default
   10%). The crossing point is the cede level at which new business stops clearing the hurdle.
3. **Back-book compensation** — does the front-end commission fairly pay for the back-book PVDE we
   cede? It shows the front-end PV, the back-book ceded PVDE, and the **compensation %** (front-end
   PV / ceded PVDE) across cede levels. Because the deal also transfers risk, recovering less than
   100% can still be a fair price.

> Tip: run the model once first so the loaded data and assumptions are available, then open Deal
> Analysis and press Run. Each point is a full projection, so the sweep is paced (a live counter
> shows progress) rather than instant.

## 8. Scenarios

Save a model run and compare two runs side by side (metrics and RBC history). Saved runs are
snapshots held in your browser; they are independent of the live assumptions.

## 9. Validation

- **Balance Plan** — model vs plan by line item and year.
- **Issue-Year diagnostic** — checks the realized cession % against the reins matrix.
- **Test Log** — parametric checks (e.g. a claim-scalar bump should move claims proportionally).
- **Data-integrity checklist** — row counts, issue-year span, projection completeness, etc.

## 10. Access & deployment

- The site is gated by a client-side password (SHA-256 in sessionStorage) — obfuscation only on a
  static site. Served on GitHub Pages from the repository root: the landing page forwards to the
  viewer after authentication.
- Precision: calculations run in full double precision end-to-end; only display formatting rounds
  (typically 1-2 decimals).
