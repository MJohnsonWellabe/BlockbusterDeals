BLOCKBUSTER DEALS — REINSURANCE PROJECTION MODEL

OVERVIEW
This model projects the financial impact of a Medicare Supplement quota-share
reinsurance treaty across two views: Predeal (before the treaty) and Net
(after the treaty), with the difference shown as Ceded.

It produces income statements, embedded value (EV/PVDE), IRR, balance plan
comparisons, and a full RBC & Surplus rebuild for the Original, Predeal, and
Net views, plus a Lift table comparing post-cov RBC ratios across views.

---

1. INPUTS

1.1 EV Projection Data (default: built-in slimmed EV, 30-year monthly)
Default is an embedded EV file (DEFAULT_EV_CSV): collapsed across product and
new-business, keyed by issue year only, preserving aggregate premium/claims
totals. 30-year monthly projection (Value000=Dec base year through Value360=Dec
yr30). Issue-year buckets: <=2019 (back book) and 2020-2030; the "<=2019" label
is read as issue year 2019.
Required columns: VarName, ck.IssYear, and Value000..Value360 (ck.IssMon,
ck.NewBus, Product optional, ignored when absent).
Key variables aggregated: EarnedPrem, ReinsPrem, IncClaims, ReinsClaims,
TabRes, CededALRstat, CLRes, TS, Comm, PremTax, LivesIssued, AEGAdminPolCount.
Note: the slimmed file supplies LivesInForce1 as the maintenance/admin policy
count basis; it is mapped to AEGAdminPolCount on load. Upload a file to override.

1.2 RBC & Surplus Data (default: built-in DEFAULT_SURPLUS_ROWS, full precision
from SurplusRecalc.xlsx; user can upload XLSX or CSV via the RBC_Original_template).
Required structure: Original section with Med Sup income (Premium/Claims/Income)
and Med Sup TSC charges (TSC0/TSC1/TSLR016/TSC1CS/TSC2/TSC3/TSC4a/TSC4b),
followed by an "All product" section with the same TSC labels, then Post Cov,
Conservatism factor, Adding 1%/3%, Total (Post Cov), and Surplus rows
(Total Surplus, In LOBs, Portion non-ins cos, AVR, TAC).
Parser stops at "Predeal" or "Net" headers so only the Original section is read.

1.3 Balance Plan (balanceplan.xlsx)
Annual income statement plan figures (2026-2035) in $M for variance reporting.

---

2. ASSUMPTIONS

  Annual (calendar year, 2026-2035 with rollover beyond):
    NIER (Net Investment Earned Rate, calibrated to plan NII targets)
    Acquisition expense ($M/year, total direct)
    Maintenance expense ($M/year, total direct)
    Acquisition expense allowance to reinsurer ($M/year)
    Maintenance expense allowance to reinsurer ($M/year)

  Treaty:
    Reinsurance % per Issue Year × Calendar Year (default 10% for IssYrs
      2019-2030, CalYrs 2026-2031)
    Ceding commission front-end (one-time, $): default $10M 2026,
      $5M 2027, $5M 2028
    Ceding commission ongoing: $200/policy/year

  Scalars:
    Claim scalar, Lapse scalar (global multipliers, default 1.0)
    Sales scalar by IssYear (2026-2030)
    Discount rate (default 8%)

---

3. CALENDAR YEAR MAPPING
  cy(by, p) = by + 1 + ((p-1) // 12)  for p > 0
  cy(by, 0) = by
  Value000 = December of base year (typically 2025)
  Value001 = January of base year + 1 (typically Jan 2026)
  Value012 = December of base year + 1, etc.

---

4. INCOME STATEMENT (PREDEAL)

  Premium       = sum(EarnedPrem - ReinsPrem)            [period]
  NII           = ((1 + NIER[CY])^(1/12) - 1) × Total_Assets[BOP]
                  Total_Assets = TabRes + CededALRstat + CLRes + TS
  Total Revenue = Premium + NII
  Claims        = -(IncClaims - ReinsClaims)
  ΔReserves     = -Δ(TabRes + CededALRstat)               (both kept positive)
  Total Benefits= Claims + ΔReserves
  Selling Exp   = Comm + PremTax + monthly Acq Exp share
  Op Exp        = monthly Maint Exp share + ongoing ceding commission
  Pretax Income = Total Revenue - Total Benefits - Selling - Op
  Distributable Earnings (annual)
                = Annual_PTI × 0.79 - (Year-End TS - Prior Year-End TS)

  IY Diagnostic Fix (year 1 only): for Benefits and PTI in cy == by+1, the
  beginning-of-period stock from period 0 (TabRes[0] + CededALRstat[0]) is
  removed from the change-in-reserves so the first projection month doesn't
  double-count opening reserves.

---

5. CEDED & NET
  Ceded line items are computed by applying the per-IssueYear × per-CalYear
  reinsurance % to the underlying EV variables, then aggregated identically to
  predeal. Net = Predeal - Ceded for every line.

---

6. RBC & SURPLUS (Original / Predeal / Net)

All three tabs follow the same six-section layout:

  1) Med Sup Income     — Premium, Claims, Pre-Tax Income
  2) Med Sup RBC Charges— TSC0, TSC1, TSLR016, TSC1CS, TSC2, TSC3, TSC4a, TSC4b
  3) All Product RBC Charges — same eight TSC labels (using All-product values)
                          plus Total (Pre Covariance) subtotal
  4) Post-Covariance    — Post Covariance, Adding 1% conservatism,
                          Adding 3% loss of covariance, Total (Post Covariance)
  5) Surplus & Capital  — (Predeal/Net only: Diff in MS income (Cumul.))
                          Total Surplus, In LOBs, Portion non-ins cos, AVR
                          (constant across years), TAC (C&S + AVR)
  6) RBC Ratios         — pre-cov, w/ margin, w/o margin

  Original tab values are computed formulaically from the uploaded data:
    pre_cov          = sum of all 8 All-product TSC values
    post_cov         = NAIC Health RBC covariance formula on the All-product TSCs:
                       = TSC0 + TSC4a +
                         sqrt((TSC1+TSLR016+TSC3)^2 + TSC1CS^2 + TSC2^2 + TSC4b^2)
    add_1pct         = post_cov × Conservatism factor (input by year)
    add_3pct         = post_cov × 3%
                       (Both additions are 0 in base year per workbook convention.)
    total_post_cov   = post_cov + add_1pct + add_3pct
    TAC              = Total Surplus - Portion non-ins cos + AVR
    ratio_pre_cov    = TAC / pre_cov
    ratio_w_margin   = TAC / total_post_cov
    ratio_wo_margin  = TAC / (post_cov + add_3pct)

  Predeal tab applies the predeal income statement to update the original RBC:
    prem_ratio       = predeal_premium / original_MS_premium
    claim_ratio      = predeal_claims / original_MS_claims
    Updated MS TSCs:
      premium-related (TSC0, TSC1, TSLR016, TSC1CS, TSC3, TSC4a, TSC4b):
                     = original_MS × prem_ratio
      claim-related (TSC2):
                     = original_MS × claim_ratio
    Updated All-product TSCs:
                     = original_All + (updated_MS - original_MS)  per TSC
                       (i.e., the change in MS is added to the All-product baseline)
    pre_cov, post_cov, add_1pct, add_3pct, total_post_cov, TAC, ratios:
                     SAME formulas as Original, applied to the UPDATED All-product TSCs.
    Diff in MS Income (Cumul.):
                     = sum over years of (predeal_PTI - original_PTI) × 79%
    Updated Total Surplus = original surplus + cumulative MS income lift
    AVR is held constant.

  Net tab applies the net (post-deal) income statement to update the predeal RBC:
    Same logic as Predeal, but starting from PREDEAL TSCs and using the NET
    income statement's premium/claims as the basis for the ratios. TSC4a and
    TSC4b remain at predeal values (deal does not change those underlying drivers).
    Cumulative income lift compounds vs. the predeal baseline.

  Lift Table: 2026-2035 columns × four rows showing the post-cov w/margin
  RBC ratio for Original, Predeal, Net (Post-Deal), and the Lift (Net - Original).

DEFAULTS / PRECISION
The built-in DEFAULT_SURPLUS_ROWS are loaded directly from SurplusRecalc.xlsx
at full double precision (e.g., MS TSC1 2026 = 1.7003958324999997, not 1.700).
The engine carries Python double precision end-to-end with no intermediate
rounding; only display formatting truncates (4 decimals for $M values,
2 decimals for ratios). All Original tab values match the source workbook
exactly to ~15 significant digits.

11. CEDANT ECONOMICS & NET DEAL VALUE
Each run reports cedant decision analytics (Deal Summary "Cedant Economics"
card; all $M):
  Net Deal Value = capital-relief value - reinsurer PVDE. Positive = the deal
    adds value (capital freed outweighs profit ceded).
  Capital-relief value = required capital freed (predeal minus net total
    post-covariance RBC) x Cost of Capital (new assumption, default 10%),
    discounted at the PVDE rate. Uses the built-in RBC/surplus data.
  Reinsurer PVDE = PV of ceded distributable earnings net of ceding commissions
    = profit handed to the reinsurer. The model is zero-sum:
    predeal PVDE = net PVDE + reinsurer PVDE.
  Cedant recovery % = after-tax PV of commissions / ceded gross value.
  Back-book (IssYr<=2025) vs new-issue (IssYr>=2026) IRR/PVDE, predeal and net,
    to show whether healthy-margin business is being ceded.

12. SCENARIO MATRIX
Results > Scenario Matrix runs a combinatoric cedant sweep: upfront ceding
commission (2026/27/28), ongoing LR-commission split, and reins% by cohort
(2019&prior / 2021-24 incl. 2020 / 2025 / 2026+), each scored under four
claims x lapse environments (base, claims +2.5%, lapse +10%, combined). The
full grid is 3^8 = 6,561 structures x 4 = 26,244 runs (use Quick Subset for a
fast preview). Structures are ranked by Net Deal Value, with best/worst tables,
a value-driver sensitivity table, a downside-protection metric (3-yr earnings
deterioration absorbed by the reinsurer under combined stress), and CSV export.
The one-year cession lag (policy year 1 reinsured the following calendar year)
is preserved throughout.

13. ACCESS
The page is gated by a client-side password prompt (SHA-256 hash check stored
in sessionStorage). This is obfuscation only: the static source and embedded
data remain reachable to a determined visitor. To change the password, recompute
its SHA-256 and replace the EXPECTED hash in the gate script near the top of the
page body.
